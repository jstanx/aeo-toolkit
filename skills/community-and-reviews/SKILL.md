# Community and Reviews

This skill builds presence in the **communities and review platforms answer engines trust
as proxies for real experience**. Engines that retrieve in real time lean heavily on
discussion sites (Reddit, forums, Q&A) and review platforms — they read these as authentic,
peer-validated signals. For many commercial prompts, a strong review-platform presence is
close to a precondition for being recommended at all. This skill earns that presence
honestly. It works for any business, B2B or B2C.

## When to use this skill

- A category where engines cite Reddit, forums, or review sites heavily (test to confirm).
- Building or improving review-platform presence (G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, vertical
  sites).
- Establishing authentic community participation that earns mentions.
- Understanding why competitors surface from community/review sources.

## Before you start

Read `aeo-foundation.md` for the category, audience, competitors, and where the audience
actually congregates. If it is missing, point to the **aeo-foundation** skill. Confirm
which communities and review sites engines cite for your prompts (use
**competitor-ai-analysis**).

## Community presence (Reddit, forums, Q&A)

- **Participate authentically.** Be genuinely helpful in the relevant communities; answer
  questions, share real expertise. Communities (and engines reading them) reward
  authenticity and punish thinly veiled promotion.
- **Disclose affiliation.** When relevant, be transparent about working for the brand —
  honesty is the signal, and most communities require it.
- **Earn mentions, don't plant them.** Unlinked, organic mentions of the brand by real
  community members carry weight; manufactured or astroturfed threads are a reputational
  and policy risk and can backfire badly.
- **Be where the category lives.** Identify the specific subreddits/forums tied to your
  prompts and show up consistently, not once.

## Review platforms (G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, vertical sites)

- **Establish presence first.** Engines often require a review presence to include a brand
  in commercial answers — a complete, claimed profile is table stakes.
- **Earn reviews ethically.** Prompt satisfied customers to review through legitimate,
  compliant flows; never buy or fabricate reviews (detectable, against policy, and
  trust-destroying).
- **Keep profiles current and accurate.** Up-to-date descriptions, categories, and
  responses; consistent with the brand's entity facts (see **entity-optimization**).
- **Engage with feedback.** Responding to reviews — especially critical ones — adds fresh,
  credible signal and shows trustworthiness.
- **Pick the right platforms.** B2B software leans G2/Capterra; consumer leans Trustpilot
  and category-specific sites; choose where your buyers and the engines actually look.

## Workflow

1. **Confirm the sources** engines cite for your prompts (community vs. review, which
   sites).
2. **Prioritize** the few communities and platforms that matter for your category.
3. **Build review presence:** claim/complete profiles; launch an ethical review-generation
   flow; respond to existing reviews.
4. **Establish community presence:** a sustained, authentic participation plan with
   disclosure.
5. **Keep facts consistent** with the entity across every profile.
6. **Measure** mentions and citations sourced from these surfaces (see
   **ai-visibility-tracking**).

## Output format

- A **priority map** of communities and review platforms for the category, with the
  current gap.
- A **review plan**: profiles to claim/complete and an ethical review-generation approach.
- A **community plan**: where to participate, how, with disclosure guidelines.
- `(assumption)` tags where source influence was inferred.

## Common pitfalls

- Astroturfing or fake reviews — high risk, detectable, and trust-destroying.
- Promotional drive-bys in communities that earn downvotes and bans.
- Claiming a review profile and letting it go stale.
- Investing in platforms your buyers and engines do not actually use.
