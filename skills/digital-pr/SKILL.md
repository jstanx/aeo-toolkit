# Digital PR for AI

This skill earns the **off-site corroboration that AI answers run on**. The large majority
of AI citations come from third-party, earned sources rather than a brand's own site, and
engines trust facts that appear *consistently across many independent sources* — "semantic
consensus." Moving a story from one placement to several can multiply citation likelihood.
This skill builds that distributed presence on purpose. It works for any business, B2B or
B2C.

## When to use this skill

- A brand's own content is strong but it still is not cited by AI.
- Building earned coverage, mentions, and corroboration for AEO.
- Turning original data into press and references.
- Establishing the consistent, multi-source narrative engines reward.

## Before you start

Read `aeo-foundation.md` for the brand's positioning, the associations you want engines to
make, proof assets (especially original data), and the prompts/competitors. If it is
missing, point to the **aeo-foundation** skill.

## Principles

- **Consensus over a single hit.** The goal is the *same true narrative repeated across
  many trusted, independent sources*, not one big placement. Breadth of corroboration is
  the signal.
- **Earned beats owned.** Independent third-party validation outweighs anything you publish
  about yourself.
- **Data earns links and mentions.** Original research and surprising statistics are the
  most reliable way to get covered — and to get cited as the source.
- **Align the message.** Decide the associations and facts you want repeated, and make it
  easy for every source to state them consistently.
- **Unlinked mentions still count.** Being named in trusted content matters for AI even
  without a hyperlink — pursue mentions, not just backlinks.

## Workflow

1. **Define the target narrative.** From the foundation, fix the few associations and facts
   you want engines to repeat about the brand.
2. **Map the trusted sources.** Identify the publications, analysts, newsletters, and
   industry sites engines already cite for your prompts (use **competitor-ai-analysis**).
3. **Build assets worth covering.** Original data studies, benchmarks, surveys, strong
   expert commentary, and distinctive points of view — the things journalists and analysts
   cite.
4. **Pitch and place.** Earn coverage and contributed/expert placements in the mapped
   sources; pursue analyst inclusion where relevant.
5. **Pursue consistent representation.** Supply correct facts so every placement states the
   brand consistently; correct misrepresentations.
6. **Reinforce across surfaces.** Coordinate with **comparison-content** (independent
   lists), **community-and-reviews** (forums, review platforms), and **entity-optimization**
   (notability for knowledge bases).
7. **Measure.** Track mentions, corroborating-source count, and resulting citation shifts
   (see **ai-visibility-tracking**).

## Output format

- The **target narrative** — the facts/associations to propagate.
- A **source map** — prioritized publications/analysts/sites to earn presence in.
- **Coverage-worthy asset ideas** (data studies, angles) tied to the narrative.
- A **placement plan** with priorities and the gap at each source.
- `(assumption)` tags where source influence was inferred.

## Common pitfalls

- One big placement and done — consensus needs repetition across sources.
- Pitching with nothing newsworthy; without data or a real angle, coverage stalls.
- Inconsistent messaging, so sources describe the brand differently and dilute consensus.
- Counting only backlinks and ignoring valuable unlinked mentions.
- Chasing irrelevant high-authority sites engines do not cite for your category.
