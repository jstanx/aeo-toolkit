# What Drives AI Citations

A synthesis of what makes answer engines cite, mention, and recommend a source. Use this
as the shared evidence base behind the content, authority, and audit skills. Figures are
directional — drawn from industry studies and the foundational GEO research — and meant to
guide priorities, not to be quoted as fixed law. The *direction* of these effects is far
more stable than any single number.

## The research basis: what actually moves citations

Controlled research on generative engines (the GEO study and subsequent industry
replications) tested many tactics. The pattern is consistent:

**Tactics that lift citations (roughly +30–40% in studies):**
- **Cite sources** — link claims to authoritative external references. Among the most
  reliable levers, and disproportionately helpful for sites that are not already top
  authorities.
- **Add statistics** — replace vague claims with specific, sourced numbers.
- **Add quotations** — include attributed quotes from named experts.
- **Improve fluency** — clear, well-structured prose; strongest when paired with added
  statistics.
- **Authoritative voice** — confident, evidence-backed phrasing (not hedging, not hype).

**Tactics that do nothing or backfire:**
- **Keyword stuffing** — actively penalized.
- **Content padding** — length without substance adds no value.
- **Unsupported persuasion** — confident claims with no evidence get deprioritized.
- **Over-simplification** — stripping technical substance can reduce citation.

Implication: AEO content strategy is "add verifiable substance and structure," not "add
words and keywords." This is the opposite of old-school content padding.

## Factor strength (directional)

How strongly common factors correlate with being cited, synthesized across studies:

**Strong**
- **Brand mentions across the web** — frequency and consistency of being named, linked or
  not. The single strongest pattern in most analyses.
- **Semantic consensus** — the same facts about you corroborated across many independent
  trusted sources. Going from one source to several can multiply citation likelihood.
- **Earned / third-party media** — the large majority of AI citations originate on sources
  other than the brand's own site.
- **E-E-A-T signals** — named, credentialed authors; accurate, sourced content.
- **Freshness** — recently updated content is cited substantially more, with engines that
  lean on real-time retrieval especially recency-biased.

**Moderate**
- **Original data / information gain** — unique research and firsthand insight.
- **Structured content** — clear heading hierarchy, lists, tables, answer-first passages.
- **Schema / structured data** — helps parsing and entity understanding.
- **Entity recognition** — being a known entity in knowledge graphs (Wikipedia/Wikidata).
- **Review-platform presence** — often a precondition for inclusion in commercial answers.

**Weak (much weaker than in classic SEO)**
- **Domain authority** — only loosely associated with AI citation.
- **Backlink volume** — weak and declining as a direct signal (though it still feeds the
  brand mentions and authority that *do* matter).
- **Keyword density** — not a positive signal; stuffing is negative.

## Engine-by-engine emphasis

Engines agree more on *who to recommend* than on *which page to cite*; cross-engine
overlap of exact cited URLs is low. Optimize for the whole field, not one engine. See the
platform skills for detail.

| Factor | ChatGPT | Perplexity | Google AI Overviews | Gemini | Claude |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Freshness | Medium | Very high | Medium-high | Medium | Lower |
| E-E-A-T / author authority | Very high | High | Very high | High | Very high |
| Brand mentions | High | Very high | High | High | Medium |
| Wikipedia/encyclopedic | Very high | Medium | Medium | Medium | Medium |
| Reddit / community | Medium | Very high | Medium-high | Medium | Lower |
| Top-10 organic prerequisite | Medium | Medium | Very high | High | Lower |
| Schema / structure | Med-high | Medium | High | Medium | Medium |
| Original data | High | High | High | High | Very high |

## How to use this

1. Prioritize the strong factors first: earn third-party mentions and consensus, signal
   author authority, keep content fresh.
2. Make content verifiable and structured: sources, statistics, quotes, answer-first.
3. Treat classic-SEO link-chasing as necessary hygiene, not the main lever.
4. Re-balance per engine using the table when a brand cares most about one surface.
